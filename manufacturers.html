Manufacturers In Sri Lanka


In 1993 a brief encounter with a piece of ‘recycled paper’ led them on a journey that would give rise to what was at that time a whole new industry in the country. The paper originated in a factory in Pannala, one of only two in the country that was making recycled paper. Due to multiple issues the factory staff had left and operations were at a standstill. Taking over the factory they built a whole new team and added new products and processes to the production cycle – creating paper using waste from paddy, banana and pineapple cultivations as well as waste paper and elephant dung.
The original factory is not part of the business anymore; instead they work with multiple smaller manufacturers from whom they source the paper for their products. Their product portfolio itself has evolved to cover a wide range of value added products using multiple mediums from paper to fabric to wood. They source a range of embellishments and outsource a variety of handcrafted work to small suppliers with final assembly, customization and most importantly quality control being carried out at their factory and workshop in Colombo. Hayleys PLC, Sri Lanka, a multiple awarded winning blue-chip conglomerate which commenced its commercial operations in 1878 as Chas P. Hayley & Company currently encompasses 12 defined sectors of the enterprise. Being the first business subsidiary of Hayleys PLC, Hayleys fibre restructured to operate under Hayleys Eco solutions with the diversifications to meet varied requirements. As a world-renowned manufacturer and exporter of eco-friendly coir based fibre products, their legacy continues on and today they are exporting over 400 customized coir based fibre products around the world. Eco solutions represent the coir fibre products sector of Hayleys which in turn is denoted by 3 sub-sectors.
Hayleys Fibre is responsible for manufacturing coir based Horticulture, Growing Media, Erosion Control, Bedding products & Mattresses.Floor coverings manufacture, floor/doormats while brushes and broom are manufactured by the brush-ware sub-sector. It all began over a game of pool when two individuals identified opportunity in the plastic film industry way back in 1996. This passion was invigorating that both these individuals left their lucrative careers and committed career suicide in an industry they both knew nothing about. This was the beginning of the Polydime journey in 1998. From humble beginnings the organization commenced operations as a polythene trading company. With just two members on staff, a second hand extruder installed in a de-commissioned tile factory and a loan shark’s capital of Rs. 500,000/- began the Polydime journey.
The Polydime group is a family owned establishment associated with the Plastics industry for over 2 decades. The company has a diverse portfolio of 6 product groups processed in three factories in Sri Lanka and one in India. Employing over 600 staff, servicing the local market and export to 35 countries both directly and indirectly. Polydime is the largest processor of recycled plastic with the full value chain from waste collection to end product processing and distribution. The company has its own innovation center for product development with the future in mind. Learn more
